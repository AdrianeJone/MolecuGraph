MolecuGraph is an AI research project that applies Graph Neural Networks to predict quantum-chemical properties of molecules using the QM9 benchmark dataset of 130,000 small organic molecules. Unlike classical approaches that rely on handcrafted Morgan fingerprints, the system represents molecules as native graphs — atoms as nodes and bonds as edges — and trains GCN and GIN architectures to learn structural representations through message passing, predicting properties such as HOMO/LUMO energies, dipole moment, polarizability, and heat capacity. The GNN models are rigorously benchmarked against classical fingerprint-based machine learning baselines — specifically Random Forest and XGBoost trained on Morgan fingerprints — to quantify the performance advantage of graph-native representations over traditional feature engineering. All experiments are tracked using Weights & Biases, and ablation studies are conducted across model depth, hidden dimensions, and pooling strategies to understand the contribution of each architectural choice. The final model is deployed as an interactive web application built with Streamlit, featuring a molecular drawing interface powered by Ketcher or RDKit where users can sketch a molecule and receive predicted properties in real time, with the app hosted for free on HuggingFace Spaces. This project sits at the intersection of chemical engineering domain expertise and AI research, developed as part of an MEng in Artificial Intelligence at the University of the Philippines Diliman.
